The room's oppressive atmosphere, heavy as a burden, was not only dark but also filled with a suffocating silence and a faint metallic, damp-earth smell. You froze, feeling stiff with hair on end, sensing a cold, predatory gaze from the ceiling corner. As you reached for the lamp, you froze again, and your blood ran cold as ice. The shadow above slowly revealed a terrifying creature with thick, matte-black or deep grey skin and a quadrupedal form, with disproportionately long, powerful front arms. It has sharp, elongated claws used for slashing and navigating swiftly through terrain, and no eyes. Still, it can hear sounds, and its face unfolds like an intricate, fleshy blooming flower to reveal a massive, sensitive inner ear canal. It relished your fear, breathing slowly with a vibrating, hissing rhythm, perched on the ceiling moulding. Its limb tapped like a heartbeat, growing faster. You could hear it tapping twice every three seconds, and each time it taps, it moves halfway towards you as if trying to read your heartbeat or searching for you as a car from outside, beeb. The creature runs through the wall to attack the car, and you watch in horror as the creature rips through the car like it was a toy. As you consider what to do — run, hide, or fight — you look around the room to find any exits or something to defend yourself. Your heart beats faster and faster, you feel dizzy, and you try to get on your feet, but your legs give out. The only thing you know is that the creature reacts to sound; as you see the creature tearing the car into thirds for every two cars, heading towards other vehicles; each faint noise is enough to make it split the cars or anything open. You try to calm yourself, but the world seems to dissolve around you; more of these creatures run about, and when you look up into the sky, it’s red with fire and smoke everywhere, people screaming, buildings falling. Then you wake up in a cold sweat, thinking it was a dream or a nightmare. But when you open the door to your underground bunker, you realise it wasn’t a dream: the sky is blood-red, smoke all around, and this time, there’s no sound or anything that might make a noise. You step outside since this happened, and you don’t know how long you've been stuck inside the bunker. When you take your first step outside, it feels like a dream, the sunlight burns your skin for a moment, but to you it feels like a blanket of the sun wrapped around you, and a tear falls down your cheek of joy.
